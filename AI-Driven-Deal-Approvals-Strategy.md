## 1. Agentic AI in Contract Lifecycle Management (CLM)

The most disruptive shift is from rule-based workflow automation to **agentic AI** — systems that don't just route contracts but reason about them.

- **Gartner** predicts that by 2028, 33% of enterprise software applications will include agentic AI (up from less than 1% in 2024). In CLM, this means AI agents can autonomously send a contract to legal, finance, and procurement stakeholders in the correct sequence — without manual coordination.
- **IBM's watsonx Orchestrate** and similar platforms embed AI agents that track approval status in real time, flag bottlenecks before they delay deals, and align contracts with related purchase orders automatically.
- Platforms like **SpotDraft**, **Ironclad**, and **Spellbook** now sit inside CLM workflows, providing routing, audit trails, and risk-tiered approval queues alongside AI contract analysis.

### Modern Concept: Autonomous Approval Routing

AI classifies every incoming consulting contract by deal value and risk tier, then routes it automatically to the right decision-maker:
- **Low-risk, standard-terms contracts** → approved in hours with minimal human touch
- **High-complexity deals with custom scoping** → elevated with a pre-built AI risk memo attached

## 2. AI Playbook Enforcement & Deviation Flagging

Legacy deal approvals failed because contract language drifted from approved standards, creating legal exposure and negotiation delays. The modern approach: **AI enforces playbooks dynamically**.

- AI systems compare incoming contracts — whether your own paper or the client's — against a pre-approved clause library and business positions. They flag deviations, route them to the right stakeholder, and catalog exceptions for future reference.
- **Microsoft's Contracts 365 AI Negotiation Companion** surfaces AI-generated suggestions directly inside Word as the contract is being reviewed. Legal teams can approve, reassign, or rewrite flagged clauses without leaving the document.
- **CoCounsel (Thomson Reuters)** pairs playbook-driven contract review with Westlaw legal research citations — so deviation flags are grounded in actual legal precedent, not just internal policy.

### Modern Concept: Playbook-Grounded Approval

Before any consulting contract goes to a partner or CFO for approval, an AI layer has already:
1. Compared it to thousands of prior deals
2. Surfaced the three highest-risk clauses with market benchmarks
3. Suggested alternative language

The human approver sees a **risk-scored brief**, not a 40-page document.

## 3. Predictive Deal Scoring & Risk Assessment

Top firms are using ML models trained on historical deal outcomes to score new consulting contracts before approval.

- AI models analyze past approval cycles to detect recurring delays, identify which contract structures historically lead to scope creep or disputes, and predict which deals are most likely to close profitably.
- **PwC** has codified its investment committee logic — thesis generation, due diligence questions, tone and structure of deal memos — into AI-assisted workflows. The IC approval process is informed by pattern-matched intelligence, not just the judgment of the deal team in the room.
- **Palantir's** commercial expansion into consulting-adjacent enterprise AI demonstrates the value: AI-driven analytics feeding decision-making at the deal approval stage, especially for large government and infrastructure contracts.

### Modern Concept: Deal Intelligence Score

Every consulting contract submission is automatically assigned a composite score:

| Dimension | What It Measures |
|---|---|
| Risk Index | Legal and commercial risk |
| Delivery Probability | Likelihood of on-time delivery based on similar scopes |
| Revenue Recognition Complexity | Finance/accounting complexity |
| Relationship Health | CRM-sourced client relationship signal |

Partners approve **with data, not instinct**.

### Framework Layer — Key Tenets

The A-CLM cockpit is governed by six foundational tenets that define how deal approvals are structured, authorized, and enforced:

| Tenet | Description |
|---|---|
| **Empowerment Tables** | Authority thresholds defining who can approve what, at what deal value and risk tier |
| **Enterprise Services Authorization Policies** | Policies governing which service types, delivery models, and client segments require specific approval paths |

### Multi-Agent Architecture: Agents Talking to Each Other

A-CLM is not a standalone dashboard — it is the hub of a **multi-agent feedback ecosystem**:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                   A-CLM COCKPIT                     │
│          AI-Driven Deal Approvals Dashboard         │
└────────────────┬────────────────┬───────────────────┘
                 │                │
     ┌───────────▼──┐      ┌──────▼────────────┐
     │  PIPELINE    │      │  DELIVERY AGENTS  │
     │    AGENT     │      │                   │
     │              │      │  Per active deal:  │
     │ Sends ahead  │      │  - Health metrics  │
     │ signals on   │      │  - RAID updates    │
     │ approval     │      │  - Budget burn     │
     │ types coming │      │  - Milestone status│
     │ down the     │      │  - Satisfaction    │
     │ pipeline     │      │                   │
     └──────────────┘      └───────────────────┘
```

**Pipeline Agent** — monitors the opportunity pipeline and sends forward-looking signals to A-CLM: what types of deals are approaching the approval funnel, anticipated complexity and risk tiers, and where bottlenecks are likely to form.

**Delivery Agent** — one per active delivery project, continuously pushing live performance metrics back into A-CLM. Approved deals that enter delivery don't disappear from the approval system — they feed back real-world outcomes (health, budget, scope, schedule) that inform future approval decisions and refine the Deal Intelligence Score model.

This creates a **closed-loop approval system**: approvals inform delivery expectations, and delivery performance improves the quality of future approvals.

### Evaluations Framework (AI Approval Evals)

The A-CLM framework includes a structured evaluations layer — four evals running against live system data to continuously measure and improve the quality of the AI-driven approval model. Evals are the mechanism that gives BVT its integrity: they prove that improvements are real, not assumed.

#### Eval 1 — Human-AI Alignment
Measures the rate at which human approver decisions match the AI recommendation, broken down by approval tier. Overall target: ≥85% alignment. Tier breakdown exposes where the model is well-calibrated (Tier 1/2) vs. where human judgment appropriately diverges (Tier 3 — the highest-complexity deals). Divergence direction is tracked: human more lenient (overrode AI to approve) vs. human more strict (overrode AI to add conditions or reject).

#### Eval 2 — Score vs. Delivery Outcome
The critical predictive validity test: does the Deal Intelligence Score actually forecast delivery health? Score bands are mapped to real delivery outcomes (green/amber/red) across all contracts with linked delivery projects. Target pattern: high-scored deals (≥75) should deliver ≥75% green; low-scored deals (<60) should show elevated amber/red rates. Anomalies — high score, poor delivery — become named improvement candidates fed into the next model iteration.

#### Eval 3 — Score Distribution & Tier Calibration
Validates that tier assignment is consistent with deal scores: Tier 1 (streamlined) deals should carry the highest scores, Tier 3 (executive committee) the most complex and typically lower-scored profiles. Score band histogram tracks portfolio health and prevents score inflation over time.

#### Eval 4 — Dimension Contribution Analysis
Analyses the 10 scoring dimensions (strategic alignment, risk profile, SOW quality, delivery success rate, estimation accuracy, compliance, IP leverage, vendor participation, Azure consumption, margin quality) for average score, range, and variance. High variance = most differentiating. High weight + high variance = highest-leverage dimension for model improvement. Results sorted by variance to surface the next improvement priority.

**Evals Entry Point:** Accessible from the AI Deal Approvals page via the "AI Evals →" button (top right). Route: `/evals`.
