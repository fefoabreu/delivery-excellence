## [AI DEAL APPROVALS — detailed demo]
*[~1:10]*

*[Navigate back to /deal-approvals, scroll to Tiered Workflow]*

The tier model is central. Tier 1 — AI acts on behalf of the approver.
Tier 2 — AI pre-scores, executive confirms. Tier 3 — complex, high-stakes,
AI prepares the executive. That's the job I do today.

*[Scroll to a high-scoring deal]*

The score is a composite — strategic alignment, risk profile, delivery track record,
compliance posture, margin quality — weighted, grounded in the playbook.
The AI follows the same logic a seasoned approver would, at scale.

Deals in the high 80s: clean, strong track record — qualify for AI-driven approval.
Policy set. AI executes. No one's day interrupted.

*[Scroll to a complex deal with APPROVE_WITH_CONDITIONS]*

Contrast this. Complex engagement, vendor involvement, regulatory requirements.
AI recommendation: Approve with Conditions — three highest-risk factors surfaced,
conditions attached. Not saying no — handing the executive a structured brief.

*[Take the approve with conditions action]*

Approved conditionally, logged, full audit trail.
In a regulated environment, who approved what and on what basis
is often the difference between a clean audit and an expensive conversation.

*[Scroll to a RECOMMEND_REVIEW deal]*

And here — scope incomplete, no comparable reference. AI flags for human review.
Doesn't have enough signal to call it. Steps back. That's responsible AI.

Graduated autonomy — acts where it should, steps back where it shouldn't.
